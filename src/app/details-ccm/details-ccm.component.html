Community Configuration Manager

What is it?

A tool used to configure data connections between our clients and "communities", which are entities that consume and aggregate patient data.

The project manager contacted me about a design uplift that would involve an update in legacy UI technology.

Existing Legacy Application

User Interviews

I did not know how people used the existing application, or how they might want to use it. To ensure the updated design would best fit the users' tasks, I obtained representative set of users to interview.

I interviewed 4 users total, with the interviews being informal and exploratory. I used a set of seed questions as stimuli:

  • "What is a typical day? Walk me through a good day. Walk me through a bad day."
  • "How much of your time is spent in CCM? Do you use it for long periods of time, or short periods of time a lot throughout the entire day."
  • "What are your goals on a daily/weekly/etc basis?"
  • "Do you feel like you already know what needs to be done when you log into CCM? Or does CCM help you figure out what you need to do?"
  • "What other software or materials do you use to support CCM related tasks ? spreadsheets? Post it notes?"

Then, I took lots and lots of notes.

Personas

2 primary personas emerged based on the user interviews

Support/troubleshooting
Primarily searches the logs for very specific values. Tries to piece together a story of an error based on a sequence of log entries for a client-conversation combination
Initial configuration/maintenance
Creates new clients and communities in the system. Also modifies existing clients and communities when protocols, URL's, etc. change. Does not ever look at logs.

Themes / Opportunies

I identified around 10 major themes and opportunities for improved user experience from these interviews.

Met again with the project manager and then the users for an importance-difficulty ranking exercise to determine relative priorities of the themes. Based on the importance-difficulty results, in which a lot of items were high importance, but also high difficulty, the team decided to push many of the items to phase 2 of the project.

The phase 1 work would be mostly focused on ensuring current functionality was represented in the new UI design. I agreed with this, as all users indicated that the current functionality for configuring clients and communities, and for searching logs, was all needed.
